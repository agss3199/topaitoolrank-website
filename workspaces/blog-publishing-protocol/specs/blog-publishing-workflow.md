# Blog Publishing Workflow: Complete Process

## Overview
This is the standardized workflow for creating and publishing SEO-optimized blog articles. Every article follows this 5-phase process (13 days total per article).

---

## Phase 1: RESEARCH (3 days)

### Day 1: Keyword Validation

**Gate Question**: Is this keyword worth 2,500+ words?

**Checklist:**

- [ ] Keyword selected from `cluster-article-ideas.md` (pre-validated list)
- [ ] Search volume ≥ 100/month (free validation: Google Keyword Planner, AnswerThePublic)
- [ ] Keyword difficulty ≤ 70 (estimated: if top-3 DA avg > 50, it's harder; < 40 = beatable)
- [ ] Pillar assignment confirmed (which cluster does this article belong to?)
- [ ] Competitor analysis: Open top-3 ranking Google results

**Competitor Analysis Template:**

| Competitor | Word Count | Heading Count | Images | Media Type | DA | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| (Rank 1 result) | _ words | _ H2s | _ images | diagram? video? | _ | Beatable? |
| (Rank 2 result) | _ words | _ H2s | _ images | diagram? video? | _ | Beatable? |
| (Rank 3 result) | _ words | _ H2s | _ images | diagram? video? | _ | Beatable? |

**Output:** Validated keyword; target word count (match top 3 avg + 10% bonus for quality)

---

### Day 2: Content Outline

**Task**: Create detailed H2/H3 outline

**Instructions:**
1. Copy H2 structure from top-3 ranking articles
2. Add 1-2 unique H2 sections (angles competitors missed)
3. Add H3 subsections for each H2 (typically 2-3 H3s per H2)
4. Identify required media for each section (diagram, image, table, screenshot)
5. Mark internal link placement (where to link to other articles)

**Outline Template:**

```
# Article Title (H1)

## Introduction
[Include problem statement, keyword in first 100 words]
Internal link: [Pillar page name]

## H2: Section 1
### H3: Subsection A
[Where to insert image/table]
### H3: Subsection B

## H2: Section 2
[Unique angle competitors missed]
### H3: Subsection A
### H3: Subsection B
[Internal link: Related cluster article name]

## H2: Case Study
[Real example]
[Screenshot/image opportunity]

## H2: Common Mistakes
[List 3-5 pitfalls]
[Internal link: Article addressing each mistake]

## Conclusion
## CTA / Next Steps
```

**Output:** Detailed outline with 6-10 H2 sections; estimated word count per section

---

### Day 3: Research & Data Collection

**Task**: Gather facts, stats, examples for each section

**For each H2 section:**
1. Find 3-5 supporting sources (industry reports, case studies, expert quotes)
2. Extract key statistics (numbers for intro/conclusion hook)
3. Prepare 1-2 examples or use cases
4. Identify visual opportunity (screenshot, diagram template)
5. Note any tools/resources to mention

**Example Research Template:**

```
## Section: "Why AI Integration Matters"
- Stat: "Companies with AI report 40% cost reduction" (source: McKinsey 2024)
- Stat: "AI adoption in enterprises grew 35% YoY" (source: Gartner)
- Example: Use case in [industry]
- Visual: Bar chart comparing ROI with/without AI
- Link opportunity: Related AI case study article
```

**Output:** Research notes with stats, sources, examples, and visual ideas for each section

---

## Phase 2: WRITE (3-4 days)

### Day 1-2: Draft Writing

**Gate**: Minimum 70% first draft completeness before moving to revision

**Writing Instructions:**

1. **Opening (100-200 words)**
   - Hook (why this matters)
   - Problem statement (what problem does reader have?)
   - Primary keyword in first 100 words
   - Preview of what article covers (3-4 sentence overview)
   - Internal link to pillar page (if cluster article)

2. **Body Sections (2,000-2,500 words for cluster articles)**
   - Start each H2 with problem/context (first sentence answers "why care?")
   - Use data, examples, visuals to explain
   - Use short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
   - Use active voice (80%+ sentences)
   - Include 1-2 bolded keywords per section
   - End each H2 with transition sentence or internal link

3. **Conclusion (150-200 words)**
   - Summarize key points (1-2 sentences)
   - Reiterate main benefit
   - Link back to pillar page
   - CTA: "Read [pillar page name] for complete strategy"

**Word Count Targets:**
- Pillar pages: 3,500-5,000 words
- How-to guides: 2,000-2,500 words
- Listicles: 2,000-2,500 words
- Case studies: 2,500-3,500 words
- Comparisons: 2,000-3,000 words
- News/trends: 1,500-2,000 words

**Output:** Complete first draft (70-80% quality okay; editing phase cleans up)

---

### Day 3: Content Review & Revision

**Task**: Self-review before submitting to editor

**Checklist:**

- [ ] All H2/H3 sections from outline included
- [ ] Word count within target range (±10% acceptable)
- [ ] Primary keyword appears naturally 10-20 times
- [ ] Secondary keywords appear 1-2 times each
- [ ] All sentences clear and concise (read aloud; fix awkward phrasing)
- [ ] First 100 words include primary keyword
- [ ] Examples and data included (not just explanation)
- [ ] Visuals noted (image/table/diagram for each major section)

**Output:** Revised draft; ready for editorial review

---

## Phase 3: OPTIMIZE (2 days)

### Day 1: SEO Optimization

**Follow**: `seo-optimization-checklist.md` (47-point checklist)

**Critical items (minimum):**

- [ ] **Title**: 60 chars max; primary keyword at start; format = "[Keyword] + [Modifier]"
  - Example: "How to Implement AI in Existing Systems: Step-by-Step Guide"

- [ ] **Meta Description**: 155-160 chars; keyword once; CTA or benefit
  - Example: "Learn how to integrate AI into legacy systems. 7-step framework + real case study included."

- [ ] **H1**: Match title exactly; appears once at top
- [ ] **H2/H3**: Keywords in 40-50% of headings; no skipped levels (H1 → H3)
- [ ] **First 100 words**: Primary keyword included
- [ ] **Keyword density**: Primary keyword 0.5-1.5% of word count (roughly 10-30 times for 2,000-word article)
- [ ] **Internal links**: 5-7 contextual links; includes pillar page link
- [ ] **Readability**: Flesch Reading Ease ≥ 60 (6th-grade reading level)
  - Tools: Grammarly, Hemingway Editor (free browser extension)
- [ ] **Active voice**: 80%+ of sentences use active voice

**Optimization Output:** Draft with SEO metadata; ready for linking phase

---

### Day 2: Prepare Media & Links

**Task 1: Identify Internal Links**

For each article, find 5-7 related articles to link to:

```
Article: "How to Implement AI in Existing Systems"

Internal Links:
1. (Opening) Link to pillar: "AI Integration Playbook" 
2. Section: "Understanding Current Systems" → "Assessing Your Tech Stack"
3. Section: "AI Integration Steps" → "Choosing the Right AI Tools"
4. Section: "Team Adoption" → "AI Training for Employees"
5. Section: "Measuring Success" → "AI ROI Calculation Guide"
6. Conclusion: Back to pillar: "Complete AI Integration Roadmap"
7. CTA: Link to [Product/Tool] page (if relevant)

Anchor text: Natural, keyword-rich, benefit-focused
- "Our complete AI Integration Playbook covers..."
- "Learn how to choose the right AI tools for your use case"
- "See real AI ROI results from enterprises like you"
```

**Best Practices:**
- Link to pillar in introduction (return link) and conclusion
- Link to 2-3 related cluster articles (cross-cluster linking)
- Use keyword-rich anchor text but keep it natural
- Avoid excessive linking (7 max; Google penalizes over-linking)
- Place links contextually within sentences, not at end of paragraphs

**Task 2: Optimize & Prepare Images**

For each image in article:
- [ ] Size ≤ 200KB (use TinyPNG or Squoosh)
- [ ] Format: WebP preferred (better compression)
- [ ] Alt text: 8-12 words; include keyword once
  - Example: `alt="custom software development architecture diagram showing microservices approach"`
- [ ] Caption: 1-2 sentences explaining image relevance
- [ ] Placement: 1 image per 500 words minimum

**Optimization Output:** Complete draft with internal links embedded and images optimized

---

## Phase 4: PUBLISH (1 day)

### Pre-Publication Checklist (Run before hitting publish)

**Metadata:**
- [ ] **Title**: 60 chars; keyword at start; compelling
- [ ] **Slug**: `/blog/keyword-separated-by-dashes/` (lowercase, hyphens only, no underscores)
- [ ] **Meta description**: 155-160 chars; includes keyword; CTA
- [ ] **URL slug**: Finalized (changing later hurts SEO)
- [ ] **Author**: Set (author: "Content Team" or specific writer)
- [ ] **Publish date**: Set to actual publish date
- [ ] **Category/Tag**: Assigned to pillar cluster

**Content Quality:**
- [ ] **Grammarly check**: No spelling/grammar errors; tone consistent
- [ ] **Plagiarism check**: Copyscape or Grammarly's plagiarism tool (confirm unique content)
- [ ] **Link validation**: Test all 5-7 internal links; none broken
- [ ] **Image check**: All images load; alt text visible; captions present
- [ ] **Formatting**: Headings, lists, bold emphasis look correct in preview
- [ ] **Mobile preview**: Read article on phone/tablet; responsive layout

**SEO Metadata:**
- [ ] **Canonical URL**: Set to self-referential (prevent duplicate content issues)
- [ ] **Open Graph tags**: title, description, image for social sharing
- [ ] **Twitter Card**: Type set (summary_large_image preferred for articles)
- [ ] **Schema markup**: BlogPosting schema added (JSON-LD in page head)

**Publishing:**
- [ ] Set status to "Published"
- [ ] Verify article is live and accessible at URL
- [ ] Check Google Mobile-Friendly Test (mobile rendering)

## Phase 5: PROMOTE (3-5 days, ongoing)

### Day 1: Google Search Console Submission

**Task**: Expedite indexing

1. Log in to Google Search Console (Search Console > Inspect URL)
2. Paste article URL
3. Click "Request Indexing" (tells Google to crawl it immediately)
4. Wait 24-48 hours for indexing

**Output**: Article indexed in Google; appears in search results within 48 hours

### Day 1-2: Internal Linking Audit

**Task**: Update pillar page and related cluster articles to link to new article

1. Open pillar page
2. Add new article to Table of Contents (if applicable)
3. Add "Read more" link in teaser section (if pillar has one)
4. Update cluster articles that should cross-link to this article

**Example addition to pillar TOC:**

```
## Table of Contents
...
5. [How to Implement AI in Existing Systems](#) ← NEW ARTICLE
   Learn how to integrate AI into legacy systems without disruption.
...
```

**Output**: New article integrated into internal link network

### Day 3: Backlink Outreach (For pillar pages only)

**Task**: Build external backlinks to pillar pages

For each pillar page, identify 10-15 potential backlink sources:
- Industry blogs that mention related topics
- Directories and resource lists (free backlink opportunities)
- Guest post opportunities (contribute article with link back)
- Founder personal site/LinkedIn (personal brand boost)
- PR opportunities (press release mentioning pillar page)

**Example backlink outreach:**

```
To: editor@techblog.com
Subject: Guest Post Idea: How to Integrate AI into Enterprise Systems

Hi [Editor],

I noticed your recent articles on enterprise software solutions. I wrote a detailed guide 
on "AI Integration Playbook: From Strategy to Implementation" that might interest your 
audience: [link to pillar page].

I'm open to writing a guest post or collaborating. Would you be interested?

Best,
[Your name]
```

**For cluster articles**: No backlink outreach required; organic growth sufficient

### Day 5+: Monitor Search Performance

**Ongoing monitoring:**

1. **Google Search Console** (daily for first week):
   - Check impressions (how many times article appeared in search)
   - Check clicks (how many clicks from search)
   - Note current ranking position (starts at 50+; should climb over weeks)

2. **Adjust if needed**:
   - If CTR low (< 3%): Rewrite title and meta description (more compelling)
   - If impressions high but no clicks: Title/meta not compelling; improve with power words ("Complete Guide", "2025 Update", etc.)
   - If impressions low: Article not ranking yet; check if internal links from pillar are strong

**Ongoing Updates**:
- Update article if new data/examples become available
- Add internal links when new related articles are published
- Update word count if pillar pages get additional clusters

## Decision Gates

### Pre-Writing Gate: Is This Keyword Worth It?

- Search volume ≥ 100/month? (if < 100, defer)
- Difficulty beatable? (top-3 DA avg < 50? or < 10 backlinks?) (if > 50 DA, defer to later phase)
- Pillar assignment clear? (which cluster does it belong to?)
- Can we provide unique angle? (competitors' content generic/outdated?)

**If NO to any:** Mark article for later; research different keyword

### Pre-Publishing Gate: Quality Check

- Readability score ≥ 60 (Flesch)?
- 5-7 internal links included?
- All images optimized and alt text present?
- No grammar/spelling errors?
- Title and meta compelling (use power words)?
- Schema markup added?

**If NO to any:** Return to optimization phase

## Success Metrics (Per Article)

**Week 1-2 (Post-publish):**
- Impressions in Google Search Console (should show up within 48 hours)
- Initial bounce rate < 70% (acceptable for new content)

**Week 3-6:**
- Ranking position for primary keyword (aim: 30-50; will climb over time)
- Average position improving week-over-week

**Week 7-12:**
- Ranking position 10-30 for primary keyword
- CTR ≥ 2% (click-through rate from Google search results)
- Organic traffic: 50-300 monthly visits

**Month 4+:**
- Ranking position 5-15 for primary keyword
- CTR ≥ 3-5%
- Organic traffic: 300-1,000+ monthly visits (for high-volume keywords)
- Bounce rate < 50% (content resonating; users engaging)

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---|---|---|
| Article not indexing (no impressions after 2 weeks) | Not linked from pillar; low domain authority | Submit to GSC; get backlink to pillar; update internal links |
| Low CTR (impressions high, clicks low) | Title/meta not compelling | Rewrite title with power words; test new meta description |
| Ranking dropped after 2 weeks | Thin content vs competitors; no internal links | Expand word count 20%; add more internal links; improve formatting |
| Bouncing readers (high bounce rate) | Doesn't match search intent | Check article topic matches keyword intent; update opening to answer immediately |
| Not linking to article | Editors forget or don't know | Add article to "Related articles" list on pillar automatically via CMS automation |
