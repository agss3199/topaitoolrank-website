# Content Quality Standards

Patterns for human-written authenticity and genuine reader value.

## Authenticity Check (9–10/10)

Articles should read naturally, not like ChatGPT output.

**Authenticity markers:**
- ✅ Argumentation is nuanced (not binary/black-white)
- ✅ Examples are specific and weighted (not generic templates)
- ✅ Tone varies within article (not repetitive monotone)
- ✅ Some sentences deliberately complex (not everything dumbed-down)
- ✅ Controversial takes are thoughtful (not extreme)
- ✅ Data is properly sourced (not hallucinated)
- ✅ Humor is subtle (not forced)

**Examples:**

✅ AUTHENTIC:
> "ChatGPT wins on speed and creative writing. Claude wins on reasoning and long-document analysis. The right choice depends on your specific use case."

❌ AI-GENERATED TONE:
> "ChatGPT and Claude are both amazing AI tools with unique strengths and weaknesses that make them suitable for different users."

## Value Delivery Check

**For readers seeking information:**
- ✅ All major questions answered (not leaving reader hanging)
- ✅ Nuanced perspective (multiple viewpoints, not propaganda)
- ✅ Practical frameworks (actionable, not theoretical)
- ✅ Real ROI calculations (not vague benefits)
- ✅ Enough detail without overwhelming

**For readers seeking action:**
- ✅ Clear decision framework (not "it depends")
- ✅ Comparison methodology transparent (how was winner chosen?)
- ✅ Step-by-step guidance (not just high-level advice)
- ✅ "What to do now" section at end of each article

## Article Structure Patterns

### Comparison Articles (3,000–3,500 words)

**Structure:**
1. Introduction (200 words) — Why this comparison matters
2. Head-to-Head (1,500 words) — 6–8 detailed comparisons
3. Real-World Use Cases (500 words) — When to use each
4. Benchmarks (300 words) — Performance metrics with numbers
5. Pricing (300 words) — Cost comparison
6. Verdict (200 words) — Clear recommendation framework
7. Key Takeaways (100 words) — Bullet summary

**Quality markers:**
- ✅ 6–8 comparison dimensions (not just 2–3)
- ✅ "Winner" statement for each dimension
- ✅ Real benchmark data (not opinions)
- ✅ Explicit "Use ChatGPT if you..." / "Use Claude if you..." framework

**Example:** ChatGPT vs Claude article (3,200 words, all sections present)

### Tutorial / Implementation Articles (2,000–2,500 words)

**Structure:**
1. Problem statement (200 words) — Why this matters
2. Readiness assessment (300 words) — Org readiness checklist
3. Implementation plan (1,000 words) — 90-day timeline with deliverables
4. Common pitfalls (300 words) — What to avoid
5. Success metrics (300 words) — KPIs and tracking
6. 2026 trends (300 words) — Market context
7. Conclusion (100 words) — Next steps

**Quality markers:**
- ✅ Specific timeline (weeks 1–2 discovery, 3–8 pilot, etc.)
- ✅ Real pitfalls with solutions (not generic warnings)
- ✅ Measurable success criteria
- ✅ Risk mitigation strategies

**Example:** AI Integration Guide article (2,150 words, all sections present)

### Business Case Articles (3,000–3,500 words)

**Structure:**
1. The problem (300 words) — What's broken with current approach
2. Decision criteria (400 words) — When custom makes sense
3. AI's impact (500 words) — How AI changes economics
4. ROI analysis (600 words) — 5-year cost comparison with math
5. Development approach (500 words) — How to build it
6. Real case study (500 words) — Concrete company example
7. Decision framework (300 words) — Scoring system (build vs buy)
8. Conclusion (200 words) — Next steps

**Quality markers:**
- ✅ 5-year cost breakdown (not just annual)
- ✅ Break-even calculation (year X where custom becomes cheaper)
- ✅ Real case study (company name, problem, timeline, cost, result)
- ✅ Explicit scoring framework with criteria

**Example:** Custom Software Development article (3,600 words, all sections present)

## Quality Validation Checklist

Before publishing, review:

- [ ] Tone is natural and conversational (not robotic)
- [ ] Examples are specific (company, timeline, numbers)
- [ ] Data has sources or reasoning (not hallucinated)
- [ ] Perspective is nuanced (not binary)
- [ ] Heading structure matches article pattern (see above)
- [ ] Conclusion includes "what to do now" (actionable)
- [ ] 2,000+ words (8+ minute read)
- [ ] All reader questions answered
